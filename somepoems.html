THE TENTH LABOUR
What to do when you are
a red thing and cannot
get the colour out from
your skin. Even knives on
the table are not enough
to peel you clean. Try to
keep quiet but feels like
culling the tongues from
new calves. On wet days
you stand by the river
and feel a strange stillness
as you both wait for the
storm. When the air finally
tightens something small
inside you does too. A pinch
behind your lung. A soft pink
edge creasing. First drop splits
you from left eye down and so
Erytheia begins to seep out
in swells. Could you hear how
it cried in the times it tried to
before? Riverbed is shifting
and now the plates inside you
sing too.

NOT DROWNING
A sprain wakes me up at the
hinge of my sleep. Here I know
the physics of my wants are the
same: a fear­stuck stillness as the
healing begins, and then I break
my own tender mechanisms in
the dark. There is a whale in my
dream, and together we are
suspended in the thick jelly of the
ocean until I find a door in the
cage, watch his body’s slow split
as we sense how to escape.
Oddly fitting, I murmur into the
pillow. Perhaps there is a point to
my pain. In my thin faith I turn to
the window but remember that
there is no one to hear, and I am
not healing, and I wonder how
his blood stays so still in the
water.
