5 October 2015
ocean deaths / natural catastrophes
five metaphors:
  1. time and its little machines of anvil fists
  2. family shirt cut with fingernails
  3. pigeon caught on a jagged window
  4. fatigue that spiders inside each apricot bitten
  5. a streetlamp’s capacity for sadness and the honesty of wine
two questions:
  1. what will the birds do? where will they land?
  2. does a heroin hit feels like a wave?
news language (excerpts from headline articles at the time):
  • megatsunami:

Not all tsunamis are caused by earthquakes
All the giant waves triggered by volcanic collapses
volcanoes might collapse and trigger
oceanic volcanoes were said to have been swallowed by high waves
quantify the size of the wave
theorized megatsunami
extreme geohazards such as these will eventually, one day happen, so we might as well coolly and realistically see what can be done to mitigate their effects

  • heroin:

    • The driving force was to test the unknown
    pumping, pushing, pleading
    And then he decided not to be.
    breathing reflex stops
    Even though he burned their house down
    Wouldn’t it just be easier to die young and not have to see your loved ones die?
    people with secrets, secrets like ours.
    came to my door with soup
    hands flashing through open windows
    girl is still fire
    teach them the secrets to better heroin
    All of that hell — even before a second

three things that frighten you:
  1. drowning
  2. finding wounds and fingering them
  3. tight cliffs
NATURAL DISASTERS

the driving force was to test the unknown said the seismologist into his tape recorder said the boy when he decided not to be -

not all giant waves are triggered by volcanic collapse but he can feel the windows as they stutter and swell under the wind - himself a pigeon caught in

jagged glass, fingernails slicing to feathers down somewhere mothers can’t come to the door with soup where breathing reflex stops where

a theorized megatsunami swallows its own high waves by pumping, pushing, pleading, fatigued - spidering through the wet until every

family shirt is soaked red and as they paused to quantify the size of the wave to weigh those quiet dancing ocean deaths the seismologists

wondered where do all the birds land and how strange all of that hell sliced through the air even before a second
